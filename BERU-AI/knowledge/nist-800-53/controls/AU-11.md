---
family: AU
family_name: Audit and Accountability
id: AU-11
name: Audit Record Retention
---

question: "Are audit records retained long enough to support after-the-fact investigations and to meet regulatory and organizational requirements?"

description: >
  The organization retains audit records for an organization-defined time period
  consistent with records retention policy to provide support for after-the-fact
  investigations of incidents and to meet regulatory and organizational information
  retention requirements. Retention is enforced both for the immediately accessible
  hot tier (e.g., SIEM, search index) and any cold-tier archive (e.g., S3 with object
  lock or equivalent immutability), and the chosen retention window must be
  defensible against the audit, legal, and incident-response use cases the program
  expects to support.

enhancements:
  - id: AU-11(1)
    name: Long-Term Retrieval Capability
    description: >
      The organization employs an audit record retention capability that supports
      the long-term retrieval of audit records. Cold-tier archive media must remain
      readable and searchable across the full retention window, including after
      format or platform migrations.

evidence_typical:
  - Retention policy documented in the SSP with specific time periods per record type
  - Hot-tier retention enforced via tooling configuration (kube-apiserver --audit-log-maxage,
    SIEM index lifecycle, CloudWatch Logs retention policy, etc.)
  - Cold-tier archive configured with immutability (S3 Object Lock, write-once media)
  - Periodic integrity verification on archived records (sample restore + checksum match)
  - Documented exceptions and AO-signed deferrals for any retention reduction

common_findings:
  - Retention period not documented in SSP or differs from organizational policy
  - Audit records rotated by disk pressure rather than retention policy
  - Hot tier compliant but no cold-tier archive — records lost after rotation
  - Cold-tier archive present but never integrity-verified (untested = unreliable)
  - Retention reduced informally without change-control record (CM-3 violation)

scanner_hints:
  - kube-bench check 4.2.5 (--audit-log-maxage) and adjacent retention checks
  - Prowler cloudwatch_log_group_retention_policy_specific_days_enabled
  - AWS Config rule cloudwatch-log-group-retention-period-check
  - Manual SSP review against organization records-retention policy

remediation_approach: >
  Establish retention period for each audit record type aligned with regulatory
  requirements (FedRAMP Moderate baseline, HIPAA, SOX, sector-specific rules) and
  organizational policy. Configure hot-tier retention via tool-native settings and
  cold-tier archive with immutability and periodic integrity verification. Any
  reduction in retention requires a documented change record with impact analysis
  and AO concurrence. Pair AU-11 with AU-9 (Protection of Audit Information) so
  retained records cannot be tampered with during the retention window.
